Building skills can change their world.

Achieve provides innovative opportunities for individuals with neurodivergent and mental health challenges in rural Colorado, to cultivate meaningful job and life skills.

Donate Today




The journey starts with becoming empowered.

Neurodivergent individuals face significant employment barriers, with up to 90% of autistic adults unemployed or underemployed. Limited opportunities increase mental health risks such as depression, resulting in a 46% higher suicide attempt rate than neurotypical peers.

Despite workforce shortages in the area’s hospitality industry, many businesses overlook neurodivergent talent due to lack of training resources and understanding of the benefits of an inclusive workforce. Achieve bridges this gap by equipping participants with skills, educating employers, and fostering workplace inclusion.

Stories & Impact

"I hope to make it clear just how positive of an experience it was for [my son] to work with you over the summer. Having the job helped me to see a more mature, more responsible side of my [son]. He gained a newfound confidence that I have not seen in him before.”

"The program helped reduce my social anxiety”

“I met one of Achieve’s program participants who is now working at a restaurant. He told me he wouldn’t have had the confidence to even apply for the job had it not been for Achieve’s program.”
– Community Member

"This is my first job and it really helped me ..."
– October 31, 2023 article in the Tenderfoot Times by Kendra Poplin

Impact: At Achieve, we measure our impact by tracking the employment outcomes of our program graduates. Since our founding in 2017, over 75% of participants aged 18 and older have secured meaningful employment. This accomplishment is especially significant when compared to the National Autism Indicators Report (Drexel University, 2015), which found that only 58% of young adults with autism in their early 20s have ever held a job, let alone one that is meaningful.
